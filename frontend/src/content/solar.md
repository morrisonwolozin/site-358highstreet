# Solar Generation

## Overview

Electricity for both dwelling units is supplied in whole or in part from solar photovoltaic generation owned by the building owner. The arrangement uses two separate arrays at two locations: an existing offsite array in Palermo that serves Unit 1, and a new onsite array under contract for installation that will serve Unit 2. In both cases the owner retains ownership of the generation assets; tenants are billed for their consumption at a rate equal to 50% of the prevailing CMP retail rate.

---

## Offsite Array — Unit 1

A 15 kW rooftop array located at the owner's residence in Palermo, Maine has supplied Unit 1 since the building was occupied. The array was installed prior to this project and generates excess production beyond the residence's own needs; that surplus is applied to Unit 1's consumption through CMP's net metering program.

Under normal conditions the offsite array produces sufficient surplus to cover Unit 1's annual consumption. A seasonal shortfall occurs during winter months when reduced insolation and higher consumption converge; this shortfall is drawn from the grid at standard CMP rates. Future iterations of this page will quantify the annual generation surplus and seasonal deficit.

## Onsite Array — Unit 2

An 8.36 kW rooftop array is under contract for installation at 358 High Street by [Revision Energy](https://revisionenergy.com), a Maine-based, employee-owned solar contractor operating since 2003. The array will be interconnected with CMP under net metering and dedicated to offsetting Unit 2's consumption.

### Array Layout

Panels are distributed across two south-southeast facing roof planes:

| Roof Plane | Panel Count |
|---|---|
| Addition roof (gable end) | 6 |
| Main roof | 13 |
| **Total** | **19** |

A cedar treeline on the south side of the property was trimmed prior to the system design to improve solar access and site efficiency.

| Metric | Value |

The seasonal production profile shows the expected pattern for Maine: generation peaks from May through August and falls well below consumption from November through February, with net metering banking summer surplus to offset the winter deficit.

## Tenant Billing

Both units are billed for solar-sourced electricity at a rate of **50% of the prevailing CMP retail energy rate** ($/kWh), exclusive of fixed customer charges and transmission and distribution components. This rate reflects the owner's intent to share the economic benefit of solar generation with tenants while recovering a portion of the capital investment in the arrays.

Unit 1 tenants are billed directly through the owner's CMP account based on metered consumption. Unit 2 tenants will receive a rent credit reflecting net metering proceeds once the onsite array is operational.
